AAR — Institutional Governance Architecture

Normative control of AI outputs and operational states before release or execution

Executive Overview

AAR is an institutional governance architecture that enforces normative control over Artificial Intelligence (AI) outputs and operational states before release or execution in regulated and high-responsibility environments.

AAR integrates complementary control regimes to ensure that AI systems operate within institution-defined rules, thresholds, and authorized boundaries, with full traceability and audit readiness.

What AAR Delivers

  • Normative enforcement before release or execution of AI outputs and decisions
  • Predictable enforcement outcomes aligned to institution-defined baselines and authorized envelopes
  • Graduated control actions: approval, reconvergence, safe redirection, or blocking
  • Institution-owned audit trails supporting compliance, accountability, and inspection
  • Model-agnostic integration suitable for sovereign and enterprise deployments

Governance Model

AAR operates under institutional authority.

Normative criteria are supplied by the adopting institution through pre-existing repositories such as compliance rule sets, internal policies, contractual constraints, and regulatory requirements.

Audit trail custody, retention, and integrity controls remain under institutional governance.

How AAR Operates

1) Pre-Decision Governance Regime — AAR Core

AAR Core operates between AI-generated outputs and their release or execution layer. It evaluates outputs prior to release, verifies conformity against active normative parameters, and resolves outcomes through controlled actions:

  • Approve and release outputs that meet governance criteria
  • Reconverge outputs through controlled internal reformulation prior to release
  • Block release when outputs exceed authorized limits and trigger controlled institutional notification or authorization workflows
2) Executable Operational Control Regime — Hard Decision

Hard Decision continuously monitors technical signals, computes objective deviation metrics against institution-defined baselines and authorized operational envelopes, classifies severity, and executes graduated containment actions:

  • Continue execution within acceptable deviation thresholds
  • Operational reconvergence through controlled system-level adjustment
  • Redirect execution to predefined safe modes or alternative modules
  • Temporarily block execution and/or outputs when thresholds are exceeded

Deployment Characteristics

  • Suitable for sovereign, regulated, and high-assurance environments
  • Compatible with private cloud, on-premise, and sovereign cloud operating models
  • Designed for integration into existing GRC, compliance, and operational control stacks

Intended Domains

  • Healthcare operational safeguards and regulated clinical systems
  • Regulated enterprise AI deployments
  • Public-sector and sovereign digital services
